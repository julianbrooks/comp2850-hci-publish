COMP2850 • Human-Computer Interaction

Participant Information Sheet (PIS) — HCI Evaluation Study

Study Title: [e.g., Accessibility Evaluation of Task Management Interface] Researcher(s): [Your Name], [Student ID] Module: COMP2850 Human-Computer Interaction Institution: School of Computing, University of Leeds Ethics Approval: [If applicable: Ethics Reference Number] Version: 1.0 Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]

Invitation

You are being invited to participate in a research study as part of the COMP2850 Human-Computer Interaction module at the University of Leeds. Before you decide whether to participate, it is important that you understand why the research is being conducted and what it will involve. Please take time to read the following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish. Please ask if there is anything that is not clear or if you would like more information.

Thank you for considering participation in this study.

1. What is the Purpose of the Study?

This study aims to evaluate the accessibility and usability of a task management web application. Specifically, we are investigating:

  • How people with diverse needs (keyboard-only navigation, screen reader use, cognitive differences) interact with the interface
  • Barriers to task completion (adding, editing, deleting tasks)
  • Effectiveness of accessibility features (ARIA labels, error messages, focus management)

Context: This research forms part of a taught module assessment. Findings will inform interface redesign and contribute to understanding of accessible web application design patterns.

Duration: The study will run from [Start Date] to [End Date] (approximately [X] weeks).

2. Why Have I Been Invited?

You have been invited because:

  • You are a student or staff member at the University of Leeds
  • You are aged 18 or over
  • You have experience using web applications or assistive technologies (or are willing to test with provided tools)

Recruitment: We aim to recruit 8-12 participants representing diverse interaction needs (keyboard users, screen reader users, people with cognitive differences, neurotypical participants).

3. Do I Have to Take Part?

No. Participation is entirely voluntary. You are free to:

  • Decline to participate without giving a reason
  • Withdraw from the study at any time up to [withdrawal deadline, e.g., 14 days after participation] without penalty
  • Refuse to answer specific questions

Impact of non-participation: Your decision will not affect your academic standing, relationship with staff, or access to services.

4. What Will Happen if I Take Part?

Session Structure (Approximately 30-45 minutes)

Before the session (5 minutes):

  • You will be asked to read this information sheet and sign the consent form
  • The researcher will answer any questions you have

During the session (25-35 minutes):

  • Task-based evaluation: You will be asked to complete 3-5 realistic tasks using a web-based task manager (e.g., “Add a new task called ‘Buy milk’”)
  • Think-aloud protocol: You will be encouraged to verbalise your thoughts as you work (“I’m looking for the Add button…”)
  • Observation: The researcher will take notes on task completion times, errors, and navigation patterns
  • Optional brief interview (5 minutes): The researcher may ask follow-up questions about your experience

After the session (5 minutes):

  • Debrief and opportunity to ask questions
  • Confirmation of data handling

What Will Be Recorded?

With your consent, we will collect:

  • Task performance data: Completion times, error counts, success/failure
  • Interaction logs: Anonymised server logs (button clicks, page navigation, timestamps)
  • Observation notes: Researcher notes on difficulties encountered, navigation strategies
  • Optional: Audio recording of think-aloud protocol (only if you consent separately)

We will NOT collect:

  • Your name (you will be assigned a pseudonym: P1, P2, etc.)
  • Student ID number
  • Email address
  • Video recordings of your face
  • Screenshots showing personal information

5. What Are the Possible Risks and Benefits?

Potential Risks

Minimal risk is anticipated. Possible minor discomforts:

  • Time commitment: 45 minutes (compensated with [if applicable: course credit, £10 voucher, or acknowledgement in report])
  • Frustration: Some tasks may be deliberately difficult to expose usability issues (you may stop at any time)
  • Data security: Although we use secure storage, no data transmission is 100% risk-free (see Data Protection section)

Mitigation:

  • You may take breaks or withdraw at any time
  • All data will be pseudonymised immediately
  • Data stored on encrypted university systems

Potential Benefits

To you:

  • Contribution to accessible technology research
  • Insight into your own interaction patterns and accessibility needs
  • [If applicable: £10 Amazon voucher / course credit]

To others:

  • Improved understanding of accessibility barriers in web applications
  • Evidence-based recommendations for inclusive interface design
  • Contribution to HCI education and research

6. Will My Taking Part Be Kept Confidential?

Yes. All information collected will be kept strictly confidential and managed in accordance with UK GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Data Protection

Legal basis for processing: Your consent (GDPR Article 6(1)(a)) and legitimate interests (GDPR Article 6(1)(f) — university research).

Data controller: University of Leeds Data Protection Officer: dpo@leeds.ac.uk

Pseudonymisation

  • You will be assigned a unique identifier (e.g., P1, P2) at the start
  • Your name will not appear in any research notes, reports, or publications
  • A key file linking identifiers to names will be stored separately in a password-protected file accessible only to the researcher

Data Sharing

Who will see my data?

  • Primary researcher (yourself)
  • Academic supervisor (module teaching staff, for assessment purposes)
  • University ethics committee (if audited, for compliance checks only)

Will data be shared outside the university?

  • Anonymised findings may be published in:

    • Academic portfolio (publicly accessible)
    • Module coursework submissions (seen by markers, external examiners)
    • Research publications (if study is extended beyond module — separate consent required)

  • Identifiable data (consent forms, key files) will never be shared outside the university

7. What Will Happen to the Results of the Study?

Results will be used for:

  1. Module assessment: Findings will contribute to COMP2850 coursework (Gradescope submissions, portfolio)
  2. Educational purposes: Anonymised excerpts may be used in teaching examples (with additional consent)
  3. Potential publication: If results are significant, the researcher may seek to publish in academic conferences (you will be contacted for separate consent)

Access to Results

  • You may request a summary of findings by emailing the researcher after [end date]
  • Results will be available in the researcher’s public portfolio (anonymised) from [portfolio publication date]

10. What Happens if I Want to Withdraw?

You may withdraw from the study at any time without giving a reason, up to [X days, e.g., 14 days] after your participation session.

To withdraw:

  1. Email the researcher at [researcher-email@leeds.ac.uk]
